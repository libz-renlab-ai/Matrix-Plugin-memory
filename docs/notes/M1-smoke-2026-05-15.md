### Finding (subtle, for M2)

When a fresh rule with `confidence_hint >= 0.9` is inserted (prior wilson 0.6) and then receives a single hit, Wilson recomputes against (hits=1, misses=0), giving 0.21 — **lower than the prior**. The rule effectively gets downgraded on its first hit, requiring ~5 hits to recover to canonical (wilson ≥ 0.7).

This is mathematically correct (Wilson 95% CI lower bound is wide for n=1) but counterintuitive UX. In M2 consider:

- `wilson_effective = max(wilson_lower, prior)` until n reaches some threshold (e.g., n >= 5)
- Or: track prior separately and blend
- Or: use Beta-Binomial posterior with hint as alpha/beta seed

For M1 this is acceptable — rules require accumulating real hits to reach block tier, and that's the design intent.
